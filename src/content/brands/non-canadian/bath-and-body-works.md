### Brand History and Entry Into Canada

Bath & Body Works was founded in 1990 in New Albany, Ohio, as part of Limited Brands (now L Brands). The company operated exclusively in the United States for its first 18 years before beginning international expansion. In July 2008, Bath & Body Works announced it would open six Canadian stores that fall, marking the brand's first venture outside American borders. The initial locations opened at West Edmonton Mall, Fairview Mall and Yorkdale Shopping Centre in Toronto, Erin Mills Town Centre in Mississauga, Stone Road Mall in Guelph, and White Oaks Mall in London, Ontario.

The Canadian expansion followed Limited Brands' 2007 acquisition of La Senza, a Canadian lingerie retailer. Leslie Wexner, then chairman and CEO, explicitly cited this acquisition as creating a "stepping stone into international expansion," suggesting the La Senza purchase provided operational knowledge about Canadian retail operations and real estate markets.

Those first six stores employed approximately 250 full-time and part-time workers. The Canadian presence has since grown to over 100 company-operated locations, making Canada the brand's largest international market by store count. All Canadian stores remain company-owned rather than franchised, unlike Bath & Body Works locations in other international markets.

### Returns, Warranties, and Customer Experience

Bath & Body Works maintains a 90-day return window for most products. Items can be returned to any company-operated store or shipped back by mail for online purchases. In-store returns of online orders receive refunds at the current selling price, which can differ from the purchase price if the item has since gone on sale or returned to full retail pricing.

The return policy excludes gift cards, items marked as final sale, and products purchased from unauthorized third-party sellers. Shipping charges from online orders are non-refundable. For mail returns, customers cover return shipping costs, and refunds process within 21 days of the company receiving returned items. The shipping confirmation email serves as proof of purchase if original receipts are unavailable.

Made-to-order or personalized items cannot be returned unless damaged or incorrect upon arrival. This applies to seasonal offerings like monogrammed products occasionally available during holiday periods.

Store staff generally accommodate returns without receipts if customers can provide order confirmation emails, account information, or credit card statements showing the original purchase. This flexibility addresses situations where gift recipients need to exchange items without knowing purchase details.

The Canadian website at bathandbodyworks.ca operates separately from the U.S. site, with pricing displayed in Canadian dollars and checkout processing through Canadian payment systems.
